Who We Are

Product, mission, and market

Blitzbewerbung is a B2C SaaS platform designed to help students and young people in Switzerland find and apply for apprenticeships (Lehrstellen) faster and more effectively. The Swiss vocational training system places roughly 60,000 apprenticeship contracts each year — nearly two-thirds of all school leavers. Despite this volume, the application process remains fragmented, manual, and time-consuming for applicants.

Our platform aggregates listings from 40+ Swiss job portals, generates personalised AI cover letters for each role, finds the hiring contact's email address, and sends the application on behalf of the user — all from a single interface. Users can apply to multiple positions in minutes rather than hours.

The product is available in German and English, covers all 26 Swiss cantons, and serves users from school age through early career. Pricing follows a freemium model: a free tier with limited daily applications, and Pro/Student paid tiers.

End-to-End Data Flow

What happens from search to sent application
1

User searches for apprenticeships

User enters job title (e.g. "Informatiker EFZ") and canton. The platform queries SerpAPI / Google Jobs with these terms. Results are cached for 24 hours per (query, canton) pair to avoid redundant API calls.

2

Contact email lookup

For each result, the platform attempts to find a direct hiring contact email by scanning the company's public website. No login-protected or private data is accessed. Results are cached per company domain.

3

AI cover letter generation

User clicks Apply or Auto-Apply. The platform sends the job listing description, company name, role, and the user's own personal pitch (written by the user in their profile) to the Claude API. The response is a single cover letter. No data from other users is included or cross-referenced.

4

Review (optional) and send

With standard Apply, the user reviews the letter before sending. With Auto-Apply (Pro only), it is sent directly. The email is dispatched via Resend from a verified sending domain. The application is logged in Supabase under the user's account.

5

Application tracking

Status (sent → replied → interview → offer/rejection) is tracked in the user's dashboard. All data is owned by the user and can be exported or deleted on request.

Compliance & Data Handling

Our obligations and how we meet them
nDSG (Swiss Data Protection Act) compliant. Data processing follows the revised Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection, effective 2023. Users are informed of what data is collected and why.
GDPR-aligned. Although operating primarily in Switzerland, we follow GDPR principles for all EU/EEA users: lawful basis, data minimisation, right to access, right to erasure.
No data resale. User data and API responses are never sold, shared with third parties beyond what is documented here, or used to train AI models.
Encryption at rest and in transit. All user data stored in Supabase (PostgreSQL) is encrypted. All traffic is served over TLS 1.2+.
User-controlled data. Users can export their application history and personal data, or request full account deletion, from their account settings.
Rate-limiting and caching. API calls are rate-limited per user account and results are cached where appropriate to minimise volume and cost for all parties.
No anonymous scraping. Every API call is tied to an authenticated, registered user. There is no anonymous bulk access or crawling of third-party platforms.
Minors (under 16). Where a user is under 16, parental consent is collected during registration in accordance with applicable law.
