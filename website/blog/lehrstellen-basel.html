FAQ

Brauche ich Französischkenntnisse für eine Lehrstelle in Basel?

Für die meisten Stellen nein — Basel ist deutschsprachig. In Grenznähe oder bei internationalen Firmen kann Französisch ein Plus sein, ist aber selten Pflicht.

Sind Pharma-Lehrberufe besonders schwierig zu bekommen?

Sie sind begehrt — aber die Anforderungen sind klar: gute Noten in Chemie und Biologie, Interesse an Naturwissenschaften und eine überzeugende Bewerbung. Wer das bringt, hat gute Chancen.

Apprenticeships in Basel 2025 — The Complete Guide

📅 May 2025⏱ 5 min read🎯 Students in BS/BL

Basel is one of Switzerland's economically strongest regions — and simultaneously one of the best places for vocational training. Pharma giants like Roche and Novartis, strong craftsmanship, international logistics and a vibrant cultural scene make Basel unique.

What Makes Basel Special

  • Pharma industry: Roche and Novartis train hundreds of apprentices — in laboratory, IT, commercial and technical roles
  • Three-country corner: Basel borders Germany and France — unique international experience
  • Strong SME network: Hundreds of smaller companies offer more personal training
  • Port and logistics: The Rhine port makes Basel a key logistics location

In-Demand Apprenticeships in Basel

  • Pharma assistant EFZ: Basel leads the world in pharma — and trains accordingly
  • Commercial (KV): Sought everywhere — from Roche to local tax advisors
  • Chemical/pharma technologist EFZ: Dream profession for science enthusiasts
  • Logistics EFZ: The port and import/export companies offer many positions

FAQ

Do I need French for an apprenticeship in Basel?

For most positions no — Basel is German-speaking. Near the border or at international companies French can be a plus, but is rarely required.

Are pharma apprenticeships particularly hard to get?

They're sought after — but the requirements are clear: good grades in chemistry and biology, interest in natural sciences and a convincing application.
